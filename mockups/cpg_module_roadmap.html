Sales & Operations Planning
Compresses: all-day Monday planning cycle into a focused exception review — the calls still happen, but they're shorter and sharper
Priority 1
Q3 2026
  • Weekly pre-fill: every forecast factor column (velocity, lift, cannib, seasonality) pre-calculated and ready Sunday night — the meeting starts from a number, not from scratch
  • Sales rep mobile view: each rep gets a one-screen summary of their accounts with Mo's forecast the night before — confirm, flag a concern, or add context via email reply or a simple tap. No new app to learn.
  • Field adjustment capture: rep input from Monday calls is logged against Mo's forecast — creating a tracked "field confidence" layer that accumulates over time
  • Forecast accuracy scoreboard: track Mo vs. actuals vs. field-adjusted forecast cycle over cycle — surfaces where the model is reliable and where field knowledge adds the most value
Goal: turn a 30-min rep call into an exception-only conversation — not eliminate it
Demand Forecast

Priority 2
Live

  • Seasonality built into the model — no manual seasonal multiplier needed

  • New SKU ramp model: replaces "12 weeks to maturity" rule of thumb with an actual ramp curve (see Distribution Signals)

Cannibalization Insights
Replaces: manually estimated cannibalization factor column in the forecast model
Priority 3
Live
  • Pre-scored cannibalization rate for every SKU pair on shelf — direction, magnitude, and trend
  • Weekly rate monitor: stabilizing vs. accelerating signals, 4-week forecast per pair
  • Assortment scenario planner: simulate new SKU intro, assortment cut, or competitive entry before committing
  • Pack-format insight: same-flavor 12pk is almost always incremental; cross-flavor singles carry the most cannibalization risk
Launch-phase pattern: new 4pk typically draws −25–30% from existing singles for 3 months
Promo Lift Engine
Replaces: guessed promo lift factor; no accounting for retailer, SKU, or discount depth
Priority 4
Q3 2026
  • Lift rates by retailer × SKU × discount depth — derived from 50,000+ historical promo event weeks
  • Pack × channel strategy: C-store singles are price-inelastic (flat lift curve); grocery 12pk has a stock-up threshold — two different strategies, one data source
  • Mo Chat query: "We're running a 15% TPR at Kroger next month on this SKU — what lift should I assume?"
  • Display / feature vs. TPR lift separated — not all promo mechanics are equal
Powered by SPINS MRM lift rate: incr_units / base_units per event week
Distribution Signals
Replaces: sales team gut estimates for new SKU ramp; no visibility into future store expansion
Priority 5
Live / Q3
  • Cold-start proxy: new SKU with <8 weeks of history is matched to the 3 most comparable past launches at the same channel and format — ramp reference instead of a guess
  • ETS uncertainty band: shows how wide the confidence interval is before LightGBM has enough data to take over
  • Distribution calendar input (Q3): upload planned store expansion (SKU × retailer × stores × date) → forward-looking TDP ramp projection
  • Data-dark retailer proxy (Q3): channel-matched SPINS surrogate for retailers with no category data, with explicit confidence notation
Closes the biggest unknown in new-product planning: what does the ramp actually look like?
Price & Elasticity
Replaces: quarterly price review in Excel; no sensitivity analysis or competitive context

Live
  • Price elasticity per SKU × retailer — with elasticity-aware scenario forecast (drag price, see unit impact)
  • Pack price norms: where does each format sit vs. the category average per bar/unit?
  • Competitive price gap monitor: real-time gap vs. major competitors at each retailer
  • Trade spend guardrails: flags price moves that trigger cannibalization risk within your own portfolio
Elasticity scored at the SKU × retailer level — not a category average
Competitive Intelligence
Replaces: manual SPINS competitive pulls; no systematic velocity benchmarking or trend alerting

Live

  • Velocity benchmarking: your SKU vs. brand / channel / format peers, week by week
  • Trend signals: rising/falling velocity by competitor brand or flavor — surfaced automatically, not hunted manually
  • Shelf share view: your distribution and velocity relative to the category at any retailer
Replaces the manual "5,000 SKU pull" that takes hours to build and is stale within a week
