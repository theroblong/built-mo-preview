The Invisible Cost
The hours you can count are only part of the cost
A planning lead running a manual model doesn't stop thinking about it when they step away from the spreadsheet. The cognitive overhead travels with them — into Monday's executive meeting, into the commute, into the next quarter's planning cycle. That context-switching tax is real, it affects decision quality, and it never shows up in a time audit. Neither does the energy drain of going into a high-stakes buyer or board meeting with lingering uncertainty about whether the numbers are right.
Pre-meeting anxiety
Uncertainty about whether the numbers are right before a board or buyer meeting. Aevah gives the team a validated signal to walk in with.
Context-switching cost
Shifting from growth strategy to data reconciliation mid-quarter costs more than the time — it costs the train of thought.
Energy and focus reserve
A team that ends the week maintaining a model has less fuel for the decisions that compound over time. Growth work needs a clear head, not a tired one.
The senior time cost — growing every year
The 371 hours per year in named tasks are being spent by senior planning staff — not an analyst running reports. At a loaded rate for that level of experience, that is $45–65K in high-value hours allocated to table-stakes work. By Year 3, at current growth, that rises to nearly $60–80K. This is not a case for cutting headcount. It is a case for making sure the most experienced people on the team are spending their time on the decisions that require them — not the mechanics that do not.
