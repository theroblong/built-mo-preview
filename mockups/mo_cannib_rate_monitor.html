What this replaces in Connor's model: The manually estimated cannibalization factor column — currently filled by judgment call or historical guess. Mo pre-calculates this for every retailer × SKU combination where sufficient SPINS data exists (≥13 weeks). Export to CSV for direct paste into the existing Excel model.
Pattern finding from 39 weeks of Publix SPINS data:
When BUILT launches a new 4pk SKU at Publix, existing singles lose 25–30% of weekly base volume during the first 3 months of distribution. By month 6, the relationship stabilizes to −5% to −15% as distribution matures and each SKU finds its consumer. This pattern is visible for Built Puff Cookie Dough Chunk Protein Bar 1.55 Oz (4 Pk) → Built Puff Salted Caramel Protein Bar 1.41 Oz, Built Puff Coconut Protein Bar 1.41 Oz (4 Pk) → Built Puff Salted Caramel Protein Bar 1.41 Oz, and Built Puff Brownie Batter Protein Bar 1.41 Oz (4 Pk) → Built Puff Salted Caramel Protein Bar 1.41 Oz — all three show the same launch-phase curve.
Connor's use: Budget −25% on existing singles for the first quarter after any new 4pk launches at the same account. This is now a data-derived input, not a guess.
