Data Intake and Governance

Govern
Executive view

Confirm that the 97M-record SPINS datasource is complete, stable, and fit to become an enterprise analytics asset. This stage protects the organization from building models on malformed, incomplete, or poorly governed source data.

Exit criteriaDruid datasource is queryable, complete, permissioned, and ready for normalization.
Technical work
  • Confirm datasource name, grain, time column, row count, retention, and segment health.
  • Verify the source grain is UPC x geography/account/channel x week.
  • Preserve all raw demand, price, promo, distribution, product, and retail fields.
  • Avoid filtering to BUILT-only at ingestion; competitor and category history are required.
  • Register ownership, refresh cadence, source lineage, and access rules.

Source Data Quality and Field Audit

Quality gate
Executive view

Before modeling starts, the team validates that the fields needed to explain demand transfer and price response are present and trustworthy. This is the quality gate that prevents black-box or misleading outputs later.

Exit criteriaField coverage and quality report is signed off, with known limitations captured as model guardrails.
Technical work
  • Audit UPC, brand, description, account/channel, geography, week end date, units, base units, dollars, TDP, ARP, promo fields, first week selling, pack count, flavor, and store-selling measures.
  • Check null rates, duplicate keys, negative values, impossible prices, missing pack counts, and inconsistent UPC formatting.
  • Compare row counts by week, geography, brand, and category to detect ingestion gaps.
  • Flag military/DECA or other special channels if they should be suppressed.

Product, Market, Calendar, and Event Enrichment

Prepare
Executive view

The raw SPINS feed is converted into business language: product families, pack ladders, specific flavors, retail accounts, market hierarchies, calendar seasonality, and known product events. This lets Mo explain why something happened, not just that it happened.

Exit criteriaEnriched weekly panel can support stable joins, comparison pool generation, seasonality controls, and explanation metadata.
Technical work
  • Load product master and similarity mappings for BUILT and eligible competitors.
  • Attach normalized flavor, flavor family, brand line, pack count, pack-size band, protein attributes, competitor tier, and substitute group.
  • Load market hierarchy: account, banner, channel, region, geography type.
  • Load calendar dimensions: fiscal period, week of year, season, holiday flags.
  • Load event history for launches, discontinuations, assortment changes, promos, price actions, and availability issues.

Druid Feature Preparation

Feature tables
Executive view

The 97M raw rows are not sent directly to machine learning. Druid first compresses them into governed, explainable feature tables that represent the business questions Mo needs to answer.

Exit criteriaFeature tables exist at the correct grain, are reproducible, and can be queried without scanning the full raw datasource for every workflow.
Technical work
  • Build normalized and enriched weekly tables.
  • Build comparison pools for pack ladder, same-flavor, same-family, same-brand, and competitor scopes.
  • Build focal and donor pre/post feature tables.
  • Build event detection, new UPC, classification, and ramp monitoring tables.

Label Strategy and Training Dataset Assembly

Training data
Executive view

Because SPINS does not include a pre-labeled cannibalization field, the team creates transparent deterministic labels from observable business evidence. These labels become the supervised learning foundation.

Exit criteriaTraining dataset has labels, features, guardrail flags, data-quality fields, and lineage to source Druid records.
Technical work
  • Create labels: CANNIBALIZING, WATCH, INCREMENTAL, and NEUTRAL.
  • Create secondary labels for distribution-led versus demand-led growth.
  • Compute incremental_share for UI interpretation.
  • Suppress or downgrade rows with insufficient weeks, promo confounds, heavy TDP movement, missing competitor context, or launch-ramp instability.
  • Use relationship distance and pack distance as structural substitution features.

Model Training

Models
Executive view

Mo trains several focused models rather than one oversized model. Each model has a clear job: identify cannibalization risk, rank likely donors, detect important events, and estimate price response.

Exit criteriaCandidate models are trained with versioned artifacts, reproducible inputs, and documented feature importance.
Technical work
  • Train cannibalization classifier on ml_training_features.
  • Train donor ranker to order likely donor SKUs.
  • Train event detector to prioritize material business events.
  • Train own-price, cross-price, promo, and forecast models for price elasticity.
  • Start with interpretable baselines, then advance to LightGBM or similar models with driver explanations.

Fit Testing and Model Diagnostics

Fit testing
Executive view

The team tests whether each model actually learned useful patterns instead of memorizing noise, seasonality, distribution changes, or promotional shocks.

Exit criteriaModel performance is acceptable overall and stable across important business segments, or limitations are documented as UI guardrails.
Technical work
  • Evaluate precision, recall, F1, ROC-AUC, PR-AUC, and calibration by confidence bucket.
  • Evaluate donor ranking with top-k hit rate and mean reciprocal rank.
  • Evaluate event detector precision so Mo does not flood users with weak alerts.
  • Evaluate price models with out-of-sample error, elasticity sign checks, and stability by segment.
  • Run diagnostics by relationship distance, geography, channel, pack count, flavor family, launch age, promo intensity, and TDP movement.

Validation and Business Review

Business signoff
Executive view

Model quality is not accepted by statistics alone. Business stakeholders review examples to confirm that recommendations make commercial sense and are explainable enough to trust.

Exit criteriaBusiness reviewers approve model behavior, action language, and confidence thresholds for pilot use.
Technical work
  • Review high-confidence cases, low-confidence cases, false positives, and false negatives.
  • Compare model conclusions against known launches, assortment changes, and pricing actions.
  • Verify recommended actions: keep, expand, monitor, reduce overlap, defend price, and test price correction.
  • Confirm explanations cite real evidence: donor decline, focal lift, TDP movement, price change, promo depth, competitor gap, and scoring window.

Scoring, Event Packaging, and Druid Publishing

Publish
Executive view

Validated model outputs are converted into governed Druid tables that the product can query reliably. This makes Mo operational rather than experimental.

Exit criteriaScored output tables are available in Druid and match the UI contracts for Mo screens.
Technical work
  • Score focal x donor x account x geography x window pairs.
  • Write scored_cannibalization with status, risk score, incremental share, donor rankings, confidence, top drivers, model version, and lineage.
  • Write scored_price_elasticity, price_elasticity_forecast_weekly, and price_event_queue.
  • Assemble unified event queues with suppression rules and severity.
  • Store source datasource, scoring window, feature version, model version, score timestamp, and source columns.

UI Presentation and User Workflow

Mo UI
Executive view

The UI does not expose raw model complexity. It turns the pipeline into a clear decision workflow: determine what matters, diagnose why it matters, and decide what to do.

Exit criteriaUsers can move from alert to diagnosis to action without needing to understand the full ML pipeline.
Technical work
  • Present priority event landing pages for cannibalization and price elasticity.
  • Provide drill-down screens for SKU summary, geography heatmap, pack ladder, pre/post diagnostics, elasticity summary, promo response, competitive price gap, MULO norms, and what-if forecasting.
  • Show confidence, action labels, top drivers, provenance, and suppression status.
  • Keep deterministic evidence visible beside ML scoring.
  • Link pricing signals back to cannibalization when a price move may source demand from another BUILT pack size.

Monitoring, Retraining, and Governance

Operate
Executive view

The system must remain trustworthy as data, retailers, products, pricing, and shopper behavior change. Monitoring turns the ML product into a managed capability.

Exit criteriaMo has a repeatable operating rhythm for refresh, review, retraining, and governance.
Technical work
  • Monitor data freshness, row counts, schema drift, feature drift, score drift, confidence mix, event volume, and UI usage.
  • Track user feedback, dismissed alerts, accepted recommendations, and override reasons.
  • Retrain on a defined cadence or when drift/quality thresholds are crossed.
  • Version every model, feature table, scoring run, and action vocabulary.
  • Maintain audit trails for executive and commercial review.
