Pack × Channel Promo Strategy — Response Curve Analysis
How discount depth response differs by pack size and retail channel · Real data from 59,746 promo event weeks (MO_73)
C-Store · Single Bars
Price is NOT the lever
Lift is flat across all discount depths: 11% at <10% off, 18% at 10-20%, still 18% at 20-30%, drops back to 11% at 30%+. Deeper discounts do not drive more volume.
Implication: The C-store shopper buys on impulse — placement, brand recognition, and availability drive the decision, not price comparison. Running a 30%+ deal on a $2.74 single bar costs margin without moving incremental units.

Real world: Barebells grew at Circle K at ~$1/bar higher than BUILT — premium positioning works in this channel.
642 promo wks · Circle K, Pilot Flying J, Love's · avg discount 53% for 30%+ bucket
Grocery · 4-Pack
Moderate TPR is most efficient
Gradual responder: 20% lift at <10% off → 27% at 10-20% → 39% at 20-30% → 82% at 30%+. Every additional discount point drives proportional incremental lift.
Sweet spot: 10-20% TPR delivers 27% lift with manageable margin dilution (~$0.90-1.40 off the $9.92 ARP). This is the most capital-efficient promo format for grocery shoppers who plan their protein bar purchases.

Decision rule: If goal is weekly velocity, stay in the 10-20% band. Only go deeper for trial-driving launch windows.
195 promo wks (10-20%) · Meijer, Publix, Kroger, Albertsons · avg ARP $9.92 ($2.48/bar)
Grocery · 12-Pack
Deep deal triggers stock-up threshold
Cliff structure: Near-zero lift at 6-7% below 30% off. Jumps to 108% lift at 30%+ (306 promo weeks). A threshold exists where the per-bar value proposition unlocks stock-up behavior.
Math: Regular 12pk ARP = $26.33 ($2.19/bar). At 30% off: $18.43 ($1.54/bar) — below even a convenience single ($2.74). This is when "buy 12 now" makes rational sense to the shopper.

Optimization question: Is it better to run one 30%+ deal per quarter (108% lift, thin margin) or three 10-20% deals (27% each, better margin)? Depends on trial conversion rate and inventory carrying cost.
306 promo wks (30%+) · avg ARP $26.33 ($2.19/bar) · avg base ARP $29.59
