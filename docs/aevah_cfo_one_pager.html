Executive Brief

Why Aevah?

What Claude, ChatGPT, and Gemini cannot do — and why it matters to your forecast

Frontier AI predicts the next word in a text sequence.
Aevah predicts next quarter's demand for a specific SKU at a specific retailer.

These are not competing approaches. They are different tools for fundamentally different problems. Using a frontier LLM to forecast demand is like hiring a world-class writer to run your supply chain.

Three things a frontier LLM structurally cannot do

180B
Cannot see your data
A 300M-row × 300-column table = ~180 billion tokens. The largest LLM context window holds 1 million. Direct ingestion is mathematically impossible — confirmed by Google Gemini when asked directly.
Why?
Cannot explain the driver
"Sales may increase" vs. "Driven by +4 TDP points and a widening competitor price gap." A CFO's question is never just what — it's why, and what to do about it.
P10–P90
Cannot give calibrated uncertainty
Finance needs low / base / high scenario bands with defined confidence intervals. LLMs generate prose. They do not produce quantile distributions for a Q3 planning cycle.

What "AI on your data" actually looks like

When an enterprise connects Claude or ChatGPT to a database, here is what actually happens:

[User question] → LLM generates SQL → Data warehouse runs query → Aggregated summary (KB) → LLM writes prose response

The LLM is translating questions into SQL and narrating summaries. It is not forecasting. No model has been trained on your demand history. No domain-specific signals have been learned. The answer it produces changes every time you ask the same question — a property Google Gemini itself acknowledged: "Claude is a generative model, so identical inputs won't always produce byte-identical outputs." Finance requires auditability. LLMs structurally cannot provide it.

The compounding advantage

A frontier LLM stays static between public training runs. It does not know what happened at any retailer last week. Aevah retrains on new actuals every quarter. Every planning cycle, the model knows more about your demand patterns than it did the last time. The accuracy gap compounds in your favor — and the proprietary signals it learns (your TDP trajectory, your price elasticity, your cannibalization dynamics) are unavailable to any competitor using a general model.
